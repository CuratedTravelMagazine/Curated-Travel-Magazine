<div class="issue-intro">
The Mar/Apr issue of Curated Travel Magazine explores the magnitude of experience—travel moments that inspire awe, connection, and a deeper understanding of place. From the quiet beauty of spring unfolding in Kanazawa to the wild and remote landscapes of Tasmania, the issue moves across destinations shaped by nature, culture, and time. We follow the rhythms of the natural world in the Serengeti, look ahead to the rare spectacle of the 2026 total solar eclipse, and examine how global events—from major festivals to the upcoming LA28 Olympic Games—are reshaping how and why we travel. Along the way, we consider journeys that transform us, whether through epic rail and sea voyages or through places layered with history and tension, such as Istanbul in Fault Line. The issue closes on a personal note with Follow the Editor, a reminder that sometimes the most memorable travel moments begin with something as simple as saying yes to a last-minute trip.
