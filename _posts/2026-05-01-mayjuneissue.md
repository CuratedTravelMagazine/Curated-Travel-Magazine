<div class="issue-intro">
This issue of Curated explores The Art of Taste, not simply as flavor, but as a way of experiencing the world through craft, place, and the senses.
<br><br>
From the design-driven environments that shape how we perceive a destination to the hands that create what we consume, this issue traces taste beyond the plate. We move through the pastel-lined streets and patisseries of Paris, into the evolving global presence of Mexican fine dining, and to tables in Santiago where culture and cuisine converge. Along the way, we encounter glass shaped by fire in Murano, textiles woven in the Outer Hebrides, and markets in Jaipur alive with color, scent, and ritual.
<br><br>
These stories expand the definition of taste into texture, atmosphere, and the quiet details that define a place. Whether through architecture, craftsmanship, wellness, or the simple act of breathing deeply in a new environment, each piece reveals how taste is constructed and felt.
<br><br>
At its core, The Art of Taste is about attention. It is the space between moments, the table, the orchard, the air itself, where experience becomes memory.
</div>
